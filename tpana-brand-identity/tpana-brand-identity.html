01 — The Mark
The Stack — Anatomy
Design Concept

Three descending horizontal bars, each offset to the right as they progress downward. The form reads simultaneously as a software stack, a data hierarchy, and structured thought. Cascading cyan pips on the left margin mark each layer — the brand's signature detail, impossible to mistake for anything else.

Foundation Bar
Full-width. The widest bar anchors the mark. Represents the comprehensive foundation of technical expertise TPANA brings to every engagement.
Cyan Pip — Layer Marker
The signature accent. Each descending bar introduces an additional cyan pip — one then two — marking depth, precision, and accumulated layers of thinking.
Ghost Pip — Depth Signal
A semi-transparent cyan pip at 30% opacity. Creates visual rhythm and signals that complexity exists — but is handled gracefully.
Step Cascade
Each bar narrows as it descends, creating a pyramid of focus. The cascading offset reads left-to-right like executable code — logic in motion.

Pairing Rationale

DM Sans — a geometric humanist sans that is both technical and warm. Its optical sizing range (9–40pt) means it reads crisply at caption size and with authority at display size. The wide weight range (300–700) provides full expressive spectrum without changing typeface.

DM Mono — from the same family, ensuring harmony. Used exclusively for contact details, code references, version numbers, and data labels. Its presence immediately codes the brand as technically credible.

07 — Logo Usage
Do's & Don'ts
✓ Do

Use the mark exclusively on charcoal, near-black, white, or off-white backgrounds for full contrast and legibility.

✓ Do

Keep all three bars and both cyan pips intact — the pip cascade is the brand's non-negotiable signature detail.

✓ Do

Maintain clear space equal to the height of one bar on all four sides of the mark at all times.

✗ Don't

Never use the mark on coloured, photographic, gradient, or patterned backgrounds without an approved dark overlay.

✗ Don't

Never alter bar proportions, remove a bar, reorder the stack, or change the cascade direction.

✗ Don't

Never replace the cyan (#00D2DC) pips with any other colour. The pips are the only element that can appear in cyan — everything else is white or charcoal.
